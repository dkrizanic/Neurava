## Discovery Notes

- User wants production-ready quality suitable for a GitHub portfolio and public web use.
- User values strong UX and user quality as core differentiators.
- Potential product wedge: smart professional notebook for planning, preparation, schedule awareness, project history, daily tracking, and AI-assisted retrieval/organization.
- Landscape implication: competing with broad tools like Notion-style workspaces, NotebookLM-style research notebooks, Mem-style auto-organization, and Obsidian-style personal knowledge bases requires clear positioning and trust.
- Vision input: LLMs should enable advanced history search across all user data, including weak-memory retrieval where the user only remembers a small fragment.
- Vision input: The assistant should let users describe what they want in natural language and have the app create, organize, search, summarize, plan, or update content for them; manual workflows should remain available.
- Vision input: MCP support is desired so the chatbot/assistant area can act quickly and invoke capabilities beyond static chat.
- Vision input: The product should support tracking past meetups, notes, project history, personal notes, business notes, planners, reminders, and detailed history summaries.
- Vision input: The AI should function as a smart assistant that can operate across everything in the app, not just search.
- Vision input: Possible team/company collaboration includes seeing business people in a company, sending feedback on projects, tracking task progress, and checking calendar availability to simplify meetings and planning.

## Product Concerns Detected

- Scope risk: personal notebook, AI assistant, calendar/planning, project tracking, and team collaboration could become multiple products if not phased.
- Trust risk: users will store personal, professional, and historical data, so privacy, permissions, auditability, and data deletion need explicit requirements.
- AI quality risk: weak-fragment retrieval and summarization require careful expectations, citations/source links, and user correction flows.
- Integration risk: Google login/calendar and MCP-style tool invocation create permission, reliability, and security requirements.
- Collaboration risk: company visibility, task progress, feedback, and calendar peeking need role-based permissions and clear boundaries.
- MVP boundary: team/company functionality should be minimal or collaboration-ready in the first release, avoiding full company workspace complexity until later phases.
- Information architecture decision: Personal and Business/Organization contexts are distinct top-level scopes; many capabilities overlap, but business adds organization/project/people collaboration models.
- MVP business boundary: include a visible Business context with basic projects/project notes if needed, but defer colleague tracking, progress tracking, feedback workflows, deep permissions, and team calendar visibility.
- Business context visibility rule: no Business switcher state is shown unless the user belongs to or has accepted an invited company collaboration context.
- Integration boundary: Google Calendar and Gmail integrations should be permissioned, user-controlled, and scoped to notebook/planning value rather than broad inbox/calendar mirroring.
- Calendar boundary: Personal Context Calendar integration is read-only in MVP; Business/future phases may add full calendar support.
- Gmail boundary: Smart Gmail full search is opt-in and should not be enabled silently.
- AI action boundary: supported AI mutations should create an action record with changed entities, before/after state, user-visible summary, and revert capability.
- AI edit safety boundary: AI-generated edits should provide a user-visible preview before applying and remain revertible after applying.
- Company registration boundary: MVP can expose a register-company path, but monetization can remain deferred.
- Manual user edits do not require full version history in MVP.
- Deferred platform capabilities include advanced log system, backup/restore, expanded business collaboration, and additional polish/features after MVP.
- Non-goals clarify that MVP may include assistant tool/action architecture but not a public/custom MCP marketplace.

## North-Star MVP Workflows

- A user remembers only a tiny fragment from the past and the AI finds the right note, project, meeting, or calendar-related context.
- A user plans a job interview or professional preparation week using notes, reminders, and calendar context.
- A user summarizes the history of one project and receives a clear timeline, important decisions, unresolved items, and next actions.
